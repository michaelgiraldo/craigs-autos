We review the vehicle first so the scope is realistic: localized repair, recovering a dash pad, vinyl work, or a broader interior panel plan. The goal is a finish that looks intentional, not a stuck-on accessory.

## Dashboard work we review

Common dashboard and dash-pad projects include:

- Cracked or sun-damaged vinyl dash surfaces
- Dash pads that need repair, recovering, or material matching
- Worn interior panels near the dash, console, or trim
- Older cabins where one new surface needs to blend with aged material
- Photo-based review before deciding whether the dash is a good candidate

## How to get a dashboard quote

Photos matter for dashboard work because the windshield edge, vents, trim, and full interior affect the repair path.

- Year / make / model
- One wide photo of the full dashboard
- Close-ups of cracks, lifting, fading, or peeling
- Photos around vents, trim, windshield edge, and console
- One photo of the full interior so we can judge color and finish
