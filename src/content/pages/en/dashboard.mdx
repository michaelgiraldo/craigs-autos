## Dashboard Work We Can Review

Common dashboard and dash-area projects include:

- Cracked dashboard surfaces
- Sun-damaged vinyl
- Dash pads that need repair or recovering
- Lifting, peeling, or worn material
- Interior trim or panel areas near the dash
- Older and classic interiors where the dash needs to match the cabin

The goal is simple: the finished work should look like it belongs in the vehicle, not like an obvious patch or add-on.

---

## Repair, Recovering, or a Bigger Interior Plan

Not every dashboard needs the same kind of work.

If the damage is small and the surrounding material is still solid, a targeted repair may be enough. If the dash is cracked in several places, dried out, or faded across a larger area, recovering or reupholstery may be the cleaner path.

For older cars and classic interiors, we also look at the whole cabin. A dashboard that is too new, too shiny, or the wrong texture can stand out just as much as the damage did.

---

## Choosing the Right Dashboard Fix

There are a few different ways people deal with dashboard damage. A protective cover or molded cap may be practical in some situations. In other cases, upholstery work is the better fit because the goal is to repair, recover, or refinish the dash so it feels integrated with the interior.

That choice depends on the condition of the dash, the vehicle, the surrounding trim, and what you want the finished cabin to look like. If you send photos, we can help you understand whether upholstery work is a good fit.
