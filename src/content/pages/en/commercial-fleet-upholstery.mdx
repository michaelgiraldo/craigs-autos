## Common fleet interior jobs

- Seat seam failures, vinyl splits, and worn side bolsters
- Foam collapse that affects support and driving comfort
- Interior panel recovery where wear is repeated across units
- Staged seat/interior refresh work for higher-use vehicles

Fleet upholstery is usually most efficient when a representative vehicle is inspected first, then
scope is standardized for the rest of the units that have similar wear.

---

## In-shop workflow for fleet projects

1. Initial call to define vehicle type, count, and priority units.
2. Visual review and in-shop inspection on one representative vehicle.
3. Scope plan with repair vs recover recommendations.
4. Scheduling plan that balances turnaround and fleet availability.
5. In-shop production and pickup by agreed batch or sequence.

We do this work at our San Jose shop and focus on outcomes that hold up under regular commercial
use.

---

## What affects timeline and cost

- Number of vehicles and whether scope is one-off or standardized
- Seat construction complexity and panel count
- Foam/support rebuild requirements
- Material selection and matching expectations
- Condition differences across the fleet

The fastest way to get an accurate plan is to start with one representative vehicle and clear photo
examples for the rest.
